Where does the data come from?

All data is being read from forge.typo3.org every 15 minutes (to keep the serverload low) and from review.typo3.org once a minute.

Understanding the graphs

Velocity

This graph works over time.
The red line indicates issues (or reviews) that have been opened. It is important to understand that the numbers add up.
This means that the chart will continually grow.

The purpose of this chart is to quickly identify velocity problems within a project because you can see when less issues (or reviews) have been closed than there have been new issues.

FORGER aggregates these values by weeks - the date indicates the first day of the respective week.

Open Reviews on Forge

This graph works over time.
The chart is based on the "updated_on" property of forge.typo3.org, meaning that whenever an update to an issue takes place or a new review patchset is pushed this property will get updated.

The purpose of this chart is to get an overview of the older issues that are still "under review".

FORGER aggregates these values by weeks - the date indicates the first day of the respective week.

The data goes back for one year.

Open vs. Closed Issues on Forge

This chart works with two different metrics.
The red line indicates new issues, that have been created. So the aggregation uses the property "created_on" of forge.typo3.org. The green line indicated issues that have changed their status to either "Closed", "Rejected" or "Resolved" and use the "updated_on" property of forge.typo3.org.

The purpose of this chart is to visualize our current velocity. So as long as we close more or an equal amount of issues than have been opened, we're fine.

FORGER aggregates these values by days and will date back the last 60 days.

Tracker Types

C'mon... you know what this does :)
It only takes issues into account that are not either "Closed", "Rejected" or "Resolved".

Cycle Time

This one is a bit harder to understand.
The term "cycle time" defines how long it takes an issue get go through its lifecycle - meaning from "New" to "Closed", "Rejected" or "Resolved".

In general you can say that a high cycle time is a bad thing.
In our case this is a bit different, because we are simply overwhelmed with the amount of feedback we get, not necessarily just bugs.
So instead of aiming for a low cycle time we tend to discuss things with users, give them feedback etc. - all these things add to the cycle time of an issue.

A high cycle in context of TYPO3 CMS means that we are dealing with issues that have been open for a long time - which we consider more of a good thing.

Review Overview

This chart is pretty straight-forward.
The blue line shows new reviews for the respective day.
The red line shows abandoned reviews for the respective day.
The green line shows merged reviews for the respective day.

In the bottom of the chart you will find two handles that allow to change the timeframe that is displayed.
